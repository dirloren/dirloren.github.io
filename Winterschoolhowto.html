What tools do we use?

The winter school will use different tools for different parts of the program.

  • The lectures will be under the link https://webconf.tu-bs.de/dir-227-hvp (access code 936849). The link will lead you to a conference room in Big Blue Button.

  • The social part of the school as well as the poster presentation will happen in gather.town under the link https://gather.town/app/RUvU3CMbUgnNyQDk/ITNWinterSchool2021. Gather is a virtual environment where you can move around and meet people.

Our gather space consists of several rooms with different purposes - just go ahead and explore them and use them as you like! The gather space will be open during the whole school and can be used at any time for meetings of any size. Gather features a chat (global and individual) which persists after logout. We will use the chat to post information for everybody, so please check gather regularly during the school.

  • A small “speed dating” to get to know each other will be using glimpse
  • For the members of the ITN “TraDE-OPT'' there will be a special day on entrepreneurship, organized by the Entrepreneurship Hub. This day will be Wednesday and this event will be run using Webex. More information on this event will be sent to the members of the ITN directly via email.

Poster presentation

The poster presentation is open for all participants. It’s a vital part of the school and gives the participants the opportunity to not only present their work or their problems they are working on, but also to get in contact with other young researchers which share similar interests and work on related problems. We encourage all participants to contribute something to the poster session! Not only posters on results but also “posters” which basically state the problem or project are encouraged.  (You can still submit a poster, even if you did not indicate so in your first email.)

The poster session will be held in gather.town. This will simulate a regular poster session in a virtual format. A “poster” in gather looks like this:

 

If somebody walks up to the poster a preview of the poster shows up. The visitor is able to speak to the presenter (if they are there) and can open a full version of the poster. The presenter may also share their screen and present a few slides.

What do I need to prepare for a poster?

We suggest the following:

  • A few slides (2 to 5) to present your work, your project and/or your problem.
  • Another pdf (landscape mode) which should be your preview - this could be in the style of a regular poster at a conference, but you can also just use the first slide of your presentation. Please put your name, affiliation and some title here, so that people know what your work is about even if you can’t be at your poster.

There are no further style guides for posters or presentations.

When do I need to submit my poster?

Please submit as soon as possible. We would like to have the first poster session on Tuesday afternoon, so please submit by Monday, Feb 15 via email to d.lorenz@tu-braunschweig.de.

Info on tools

Big Blue Button

Big Blue Button is a conference system that runs in your browser. It is known to work well with Chrome and Firefox (and issues have been reported with Edge and Safari, for example) and basically all devices (including computers, laptops, tablets, and mobile phones). One you enter the room you will be asked for permission to connect to audio and then you can start to connect to video with the big blue button on the bottom of the space. The system is pretty intuitive and similar to many other systems.

Gather.town

Gathe.town allows you to move around in a virtual space and get in contact with people. You just follow the link, give permissions for audio and video and you can start to move around with your arrow keys. The system needs a laptop or a computer and does not work well on mobile phones or tablets.

Glimpse

Glimpse is a social platform for back-to-back, 1-on-1 video chats. They guarantee the best matches through their unique tagging system. Whether it’s new member orientation, happy hour with best friends, or speed networking at a conference, Glimpse keeps your community engaged, friendly, and connected.

My Glimpse video isn't working, what can I do?

Make sure you turn off your video on Zoom, Google Meet, or MS Teams and try again. Typically, this issue arises because computers running Windows don't allow two video screens at the same time.

Browsers/Devices Supported:

Web:

Google Chrome: Recommended

Safari, Edge, and Firefox: Glimpse works, however these are not tested as rigorously.

Mobile:

Glimpse is available on iOS and Android by installing our apps.

You cannot use Glimpse Web in your mobile browser.

Webex

Webex runs on all systems but with different utilities. There are apps available for Window, iOS and Android and under Linux it runs directly in the browser.
